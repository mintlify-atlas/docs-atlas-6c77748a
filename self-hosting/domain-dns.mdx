A custom domain routed through Cloudflare gives your Holy Unblocker instance DDoS protection, free SSL, and easy DNS management.

## Configure Cloudflare DNS

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Cloudflare account and add your site">
    Go to [cloudflare.com](https://cloudflare.com), create an account, and click **Add a site**. Enter your domain name and select the free plan.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update your nameservers at your registrar">
    Cloudflare will show you two nameservers (e.g., `ada.ns.cloudflare.com` and `cole.ns.cloudflare.com`). Log in to your domain registrar (Porkbun, Namecheap, etc.) and replace the existing nameservers with Cloudflare's.

    Propagation can take up to 24 hours, though it usually completes within an hour.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add DNS A records">
    In the Cloudflare dashboard, go to **DNS > Records** and add the following records pointing to your VPS IP address:

    | Type | Name | Content | Proxy status |
    |------|------|---------|--------------|
    | A | `@` | `YOUR_VPS_IP` | Proxied |
    | A | `www` | `YOUR_VPS_IP` | Proxied |
    | A | `a` | `YOUR_VPS_IP` | Proxied |

    - `@` covers the apex domain (`example.com`)
    - `www` covers `www.example.com`
    - `a` covers `a.example.com`, which can serve alternate Holy Unblocker instances or other services

    Add additional subdomain records (`b`, `c`, etc.) as needed for more instances.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure SSL/TLS mode">
    In the Cloudflare dashboard, go to **SSL/TLS > Overview** and choose a mode:

    | Mode | When to use |
    |------|-------------|
    | **Flexible** | You have not set up Let's Encrypt or a certificate on your server. Cloudflare handles SSL to the browser; traffic from Cloudflare to your server is unencrypted. |
    | **Full** | You have a self-signed or Let's Encrypt certificate installed on your server via NGINX. |

    <Note>
      If you followed the [NGINX configuration](/self-hosting/nginx) guide and ran Certbot, use **Full** mode. For a basic setup without Certbot, use **Flexible**.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Force HTTPS">
    In the Cloudflare dashboard, go to **SSL/TLS > Edge Certificates** and enable **Always Use HTTPS**. This redirects all HTTP traffic to HTTPS automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  After updating your NGINX config to match your domain, reload NGINX on your VPS:

  ```bash
  sudo systemctl reload nginx
  ```
</Note>
